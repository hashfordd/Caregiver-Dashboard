# F7 — Calibration Workflow

## Goal

A caregiver opens the Calibration panel inside the patient dashboard, walks the wearable to a known physical location in the patient's space, clicks the matching point on the F5 floor plan, and presses "Capture". Over the next 5–10 s the dashboard accumulates the live BLE / WiFi RSSI samples streaming from the device, computes per-beacon mean RSSI + standard deviation + sample count, and writes a single `calibration_points` row containing the aggregated `ble_signature` and `wifi_signature`. After eight or more captures spread across the space, F7 is done and F8 (Phase 3 indoor positioning) has the fingerprint corpus it needs to run.

## Acceptance criteria

The build spec lists a positioning-accuracy criterion under F7 — "position estimate error < 1.5 m on 80% of samples". **This criterion has been REATTRIBUTED to Phase 3 / F8.** It cannot be evaluated in Phase 2 because nothing computes a position estimate yet — the F8 indoor positioning algorithm is what consumes the calibration corpus to produce estimates, and F8 does not exist in Phase 2. This reattribution is explicit in [../PHASES.md Phase 2 Risks](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place) ("Spec acceptance leak") and is the single Phase 2 spec correction.

F7's true Phase 2 acceptance is **calibration data captured with sufficient quality**:

- The caregiver can place ≥ 8 calibration points spread across the F5 floor plan.
- Each capture aggregates a 5–10 s window of live signals from the patient's device into a `CalibrationSignature` (shape locked below) and writes one `calibration_points` row.
- Each row's `ble_signature` references at least the placed beacons reachable from that physical position; mean RSSI is within reasonable physical range (-127 to 0 dBm).
- Quality thresholds enforced before writing the row: `quality.sample_count_total ≥ 30` (≈ 5 s at 6 Hz expected signals rate; capture window automatically extends if fewer have arrived), and per-beacon `rssi_stddev ≤ 8 dB` for the strongest 3 beacons (proxy for "the wearable is stationary"). A capture failing thresholds shows a "signal too unstable — try again" message and **does not** write a row.
- Empty / loading / error / stale states present per [../CROSS_CUTTING.md §4](../CROSS_CUTTING.md#4-loading--empty--error--stale-states).
- Phase 2 exit-gate-level test: a 4-room floor plan with ≥ 3 placed beacons can accumulate ≥ 8 calibration_points rows, each with populated `ble_signature` and `wifi_signature`.

The reattribution rationale (kept here so future readers do not re-introduce the criterion to F7): the dashboard cannot measure positioning error without a positioning system. Phase 3 / F8 is the right home for that criterion. Doing so does not weaken F7 — F7's job is the **input quality** to the positioning algorithm; the **output quality** belongs to the algorithm's owner.

## Dependencies

**Hard prerequisites**

- F5 shipped — calibration points are placed on the F5 canvas; the row's `floor_plan_id` references a `floor_plans` row.
- F6 shipped — calibration aggregates RSSI **per paired beacon**. Without F6 there are no beacons to key the BLE signature against. WiFi signatures key on BSSID and don't need pairing — they're recorded as observed.
- The signals broadcast channel from F6 (extended `usePatientStream` with `onSignals` callback). F7 reuses that callback; it does not subscribe directly.
- `calibration_points` table from the foundation migration (already in place; see `supabase/migrations/20260504000000_init.sql` lines 96–107). The `ble_signature` / `wifi_signature` JSONB columns receive `CalibrationSignature` payloads.
- RLS read policy on `calibration_points` (already in place, joins through `floor_plans` per the foundation migration). RLS **write** policies for `calibration_points` are added by this feature (see Migrations).
- Cross-cutting decision [../CROSS_CUTTING.md §6 — Canvas state](../CROSS_CUTTING.md#6-canvas-state-pattern): calibration points are addressable rows, rendered on the canvas as visual annotations.

**Soft prerequisites**

- Mock generator's `signals` mode publishes a realistic RSSI distribution per known beacon (Phase 2 entry criterion in [../PHASES.md](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place)). The Phase 2 integration test in [../PHASES.md](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place) ("Calibration aggregation correctness") drives the mock with a known distribution and asserts mean within 2 dB.

**What this feature unblocks**

- F8 (indoor positioning) — F8's fingerprint matcher reads `calibration_points.ble_signature` + `wifi_signature` and computes kNN distance against the live signal vector. **F8 reads these rows; F7 writes them.** The signature shape locked below is F8's input. See [./F8.md](./F8.md) (Phase 3 — pending).
- The Phase 2 verification gate ("≥ 8 calibration points captured across the test space" in [../PHASES.md](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place)).

## Contracts in `packages/shared`

The single most load-bearing F7 deliverable. The `CalibrationSignature` shape is **locked in Phase 2** even though its primary consumer is F8 in Phase 3, because the JSONB columns persist these payloads and changing the shape later means a data migration. Lock it now, refine in F8 only if a real defect surfaces.

Path: `packages/shared/src/positioning/types.ts`.

```ts
/**
 * Calibration signature persisted in calibration_points.ble_signature and
 * calibration_points.wifi_signature. Written by F7's CaptureCoordinator;
 * read by F8's fingerprint matcher in Phase 3.
 *
 * Design choices:
 *   - One signature per modality (BLE, WiFi). Stored in two separate JSONB
 *     columns rather than a fused payload because they have different
 *     identifiers (mac vs. bssid) and different reliability characteristics.
 *     Phase 3 fingerprint matching weights them independently.
 *   - Stored as a flat array of samples, not a map keyed by id, because a
 *     beacon may be observed once or many times within the window. The flat
 *     shape lets F8 add per-sample provenance fields later without
 *     restructuring.
 *   - `quality` is the cheap summary the dashboard renders without
 *     re-aggregating; it must reflect what was actually written.
 *   - `captured_at` is ISO 8601 UTC per CROSS_CUTTING §5. The DB also stores
 *     `captured_at timestamptz default now()` on the row; the JSONB copy
 *     simplifies replay tooling that reads only the JSONB.
 */
export type BleSignatureSample = {
  /** BLE MAC address; matches beacons.mac_address when paired. */
  mac: string;
  /** Mean RSSI in dBm over the capture window. */
  rssi_mean: number;
  /** Standard deviation in dB; quality proxy. */
  rssi_stddev: number;
  /** Number of raw samples that contributed to this entry. */
  sample_count: number;
};

export type WifiSignatureSample = {
  /** WiFi BSSID. */
  bssid: string;
  /** Optional human-readable SSID at capture time. Not used for matching. */
  ssid?: string;
  rssi_mean: number;
  rssi_stddev: number;
  sample_count: number;
};

export type SignatureQuality = {
  /** Total raw samples across BLE + WiFi during the capture window. */
  sample_count_total: number;
  ble_count: number;
  wifi_count: number;
  /** Capture window in milliseconds. */
  window_ms: number;
};

export type CalibrationSignature<TSample = BleSignatureSample | WifiSignatureSample> = {
  /** ISO 8601 UTC. */
  captured_at: string;
  samples: TSample[];
  quality: SignatureQuality;
};

/**
 * Concrete aliases used at write / read time for clarity.
 */
export type BleCalibrationSignature = CalibrationSignature<BleSignatureSample>;
export type WifiCalibrationSignature = CalibrationSignature<WifiSignatureSample>;
```

Rationale for refinements over the brief's draft shape:

- **Per-modality signatures kept separate** rather than merged into one `samples` array with a `type` discriminator. The two modalities are fused by F8 with independent weights; keeping them separate at the storage layer matches the algorithm and avoids a runtime split on every read.
- **`window_ms` added to `quality`**. A signature captured during a 5 s window is qualitatively different from one captured during a 10 s window. F8 may opt to weight by sample count alone, but persisting the window length is cheap insurance for evaluation tooling.
- **`SignatureQuality` named** so it can be reused by F8's runtime fingerprint extraction (the live RSSI vector at query time is shaped similarly).

If F8 later finds a reason to denormalise (e.g. a unified `samples` array for a single-pass kNN), the per-modality types still serve as the wire shape; F8 can join them in memory.

The mqtt wire schemas in `packages/shared/src/mqtt/signals.ts` are upstream — F7 consumes `BleSample` / `WifiSample` from there and produces the aggregated types here. The relationship: `signals.ts` describes the per-tick RSSI; `positioning/types.ts` describes the per-window aggregate.

## Migrations

Two migrations, in order:

- `<ts>_calibration_points_rls_writes.sql` — Adds insert / update / delete RLS policies on `public.calibration_points`. Pattern per [../CROSS_CUTTING.md §1](../CROSS_CUTTING.md#1-rls-write-policies); the predicate must join through `floor_plans` (the table itself does not carry `patient_id`): `with check (exists (select 1 from public.floor_plans fp where fp.id = calibration_points.floor_plan_id and public.is_caregiver_for(fp.patient_id)))`. Same predicate for `using` on update / delete; `with check` mirrored on update. Rollback is `drop policy ... on public.calibration_points` per policy name; additive change, no data migration.

- `<ts+1>_capture_calibration_point_rpc.sql` — Optional. Creates `public.capture_calibration_point(floor_plan_id uuid, x_canvas numeric, y_canvas numeric, ble_signature jsonb, wifi_signature jsonb) returns calibration_points` as SECURITY DEFINER. See the RPCs section for the trade-off; if not chosen, omit this migration. Rollback is `drop function public.capture_calibration_point(...);` — additive, no data migration.

## RPCs / edge function changes

**Trade-off: SECURITY DEFINER RPC vs. plain RLS-gated insert.**

The Phase 2 design in [../PHASES.md](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place) recommends client-driven aggregation. The capture write is therefore a single `insert` with two JSONB columns. Two options:

1. **Plain RLS-gated insert** (`from('calibration_points').insert(...)`). RLS predicate joins through `floor_plans` to check allocation. Simple. Aligns with F5 and F6's approach.
2. **SECURITY DEFINER RPC** `capture_calibration_point(floor_plan_id, x_canvas, y_canvas, ble_signature, wifi_signature) returns calibration_points`. Function checks allocation explicitly, validates the signature shape against an embedded JSON schema (or just length / range checks), inserts, returns the row. Lets the dashboard call a single typed RPC instead of crafting an insert.

**Recommendation: option 1 (plain RLS-gated insert)**, matching F5 and F6. Reasons:

- The RLS join through `floor_plans` is straightforward and matches the existing read policy.
- The dashboard already validates the signature client-side (it's the thing that built the signature) — server-side schema validation duplicates that work and costs developer ergonomics.
- The `audit_log` trigger in Phase 4 / 5 will fire on row insert regardless of whether the path is plain insert or RPC.
- An RPC is the right answer when there's atomicity, multi-table writes, or a privilege gap to bridge. None apply here.

If a future requirement appears (e.g. capturing on behalf of a service-role process during automated re-calibration), revisit. The migration listed second above is the **fallback** if option 2 is chosen mid-build; do not ship both.

**Why client-driven aggregation, not server-driven** (restated from [../PHASES.md Phase 2](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place) so it lives in F7's doc as well): the project deliberately does not persist raw `signals`. Therefore the server has nothing to aggregate after the fact. The dashboard subscribes to the live signals broadcast (per F6's extension to `usePatientStream`), accumulates the sample window in memory, computes the aggregate, and POSTs the finished signature. This keeps storage cost low at the price of doing aggregation on the client where the signals live. The trade-off is revisited in Phase 3 for `position_estimator` if it needs persisted history.

## Tests

Vitest, run via `npm run test --workspace apps/web` for the dashboard tests and `npm run test --workspace packages/shared` for the type-only tests (the type module gets a smoke test asserting the shape stays serialisable).

- **Aggregation correctness** (`calibrationAggregator.test.ts`). Drive the aggregator with a generated RSSI distribution: BLE MAC `aa:bb:cc:dd:ee:01` at 60 samples drawn from `N(-58, 2)` over a 6 s window; assert `rssi_mean` within ±0.5 dBm of -58, `rssi_stddev` within ±1 dB of 2, `sample_count == 60`. Repeat for two more MACs at different means. This is the Phase 2 verification gate item from [../PHASES.md](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place) ("Calibration aggregation correctness").
- **Quality threshold rejection** (`calibrationAggregator.test.ts`). Drive the aggregator with only 12 samples in 5 s (below threshold) → assert `evaluateQuality` returns `{ ok: false, reason: 'sample_count_below_threshold' }`. Drive with high-stddev BLE samples (`N(-58, 15)`) → assert `{ ok: false, reason: 'unstable_signal' }`. Both paths must surface the reason to the UI.
- **Capture coordinator full cycle** (`CaptureCoordinator.test.tsx`). Mock the signals callback to deliver a 6 s window of well-shaped samples; user clicks Capture; coordinator opens window, accumulates, finalises, calls the insert mutation; assert mutation invoked with the expected `ble_signature.samples.length === 3`, `wifi_signature.samples.length === 1`, and quality field populated. Then re-run with a 1 s short window: capture rejects, mutation NOT called, error UI shows.
- **Window timing** (`CaptureCoordinator.test.tsx`). The default window is 5 s; if `quality.sample_count_total < 30` at 5 s, the window auto-extends to 10 s; if still below, capture rejects. Assert via fake timers.
- **Type round-trip** (`packages/shared/src/positioning/__tests__/types.test.ts`). Construct a `BleCalibrationSignature`, `JSON.stringify` then `JSON.parse`, assert structural equality. Ensures the type stays JSONB-friendly.

## Risks

- **Signal noise during capture.** A patient or caregiver in motion produces unstable RSSI. The `rssi_stddev` threshold is the mitigation; capture rejects when the wearable was likely moving. The threshold (8 dB on the strongest 3 beacons) is empirical and may need tuning against the test environment in Phase 3 evaluation. Document the chosen value in code; expose it as a configurable constant (not a runtime feature flag — a code constant tested via Vitest).
- **Sample window not long enough for sparse signals environments.** If signals arrive at < 6 Hz (the assumed rate) the 5 s window may not hit the 30-sample floor. Auto-extension to 10 s mitigates the common case; a flat fallback rejection mitigates the pathological case. Caregiver-facing message: "signal too sparse — verify the wearable is on and try again". Do not silently extend past 10 s.
- **Calibration coverage too sparse.** F8's fingerprint matcher needs spatial coverage of the room. Eight points across a 4-room space averages two per room; that is the Phase 2 exit gate but not necessarily enough for F8 accuracy. UX must surface the count and suggest spreading captures (e.g. a heatmap of placed points colored by density). Density visualisation is V2 — for V1, just show the count and a per-room tally.
- **Stale calibrations after F5 edit.** Per [../CROSS_CUTTING.md §6](../CROSS_CUTTING.md#6-canvas-state-pattern), F5 warns but does not auto-invalidate. F7 surfaces "captured_at" age on each point so a caregiver can see which captures predate a recent canvas edit. Re-capturing replaces nothing — it appends a new row. (No update path; calibration points are immutable artefacts.)
- **Reattribution of the accuracy criterion.** Re-stating: "< 1.5 m error on 80% of samples" belongs to F8 and Phase 3, not F7 / Phase 2. If a future contributor re-files this against F7, point them at this section and at [../PHASES.md Phase 2 Risks](../PHASES.md#phase-2--place).
- **Beacon set drift mid-capture.** If a beacon is paired or unpaired during a 5–10 s capture, the signature's `samples` may include a MAC that disappears from `beacons`. The aggregator does not gate on the current beacon set; it records what it observed. F8 tolerates a `BleSignatureSample` whose `mac` no longer matches any `beacons` row (the kNN distance just ignores absent dimensions, or the matcher logs and skips). Document this contract in the F8 doc when it lands.
- **`quality.window_ms` consistency.** The aggregator reports the **actual** window it observed, not the requested window. If a capture is aborted at 7 s, `window_ms` is ~7000. F8 reads this for sample weighting; the dashboard reads it for the quality readout.
